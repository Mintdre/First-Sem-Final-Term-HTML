Chop Suey Recipe

  1. Ingredients
    1. 250 g chicken breast (or 200 g shrimp) or a mix
    2. Vegetables
      1. 1 small carrot (sliced diagonally)
      2. 1/2 small cabbage (bite-sized)
      3. 1 small broccoli (florets)
      4. 1 small cauliflower (florets)
      5. 1 red bell pepper and 1 green bell pepper (sliced)
      6. a handful of snow peas or Baguio beans
      7. 1/2 cup baby/young corn
      8. 1/2 onion (sliced)
      9. and 3 cloves garlic (minced)
    3. Sauce & other items
      1. 2 tbsp oyster sauce
      2. 1 tbsp soy sauce
      3. 1 tsp fish sauce (optional)
      4. 1 tsp sugar
      5. 1 cup chicken broth (or water + bouillon)
      6. 1 tsp cornstarch dissolved in 2 tbsp water
      7. 2 tbsp cooking oil
      8. salt and pepper to taste
      9. optional sesame oil or quail eggs for garnish.
  1. Slice 250 g chicken or 200 g shrimp (or your preferred protein) into bite-sized pieces and pat dry. Wash and cut 1 small carrot diagonally, 1/2 small cabbage into bite-sized pieces, 1 small broccoli and 1 small cauliflower into florets, 1 red and 1 green bell pepper into strips, a handful of snow peas (or Baguio beans), and halve 1/2 cup baby corn.
  2. In a bowl mix 2 tbsp oyster sauce, 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp fish sauce (optional), 1 tsp sugar, and 1 cup chicken broth. Dissolve 1 tsp cornstarch in 2 tbsp water and set aside to thicken the stir-fry later.
  3. Heat 2 tbsp cooking oil in a wok or large pan over medium-high heat, then sauté 3 cloves minced garlic and 1/2 sliced onion until fragrant and translucent. Be careful not to burn the garlic; reduce the heat slightly if it starts to brown too quickly.
  4. Add the prepared protein to the pan, season lightly with salt and pepper, and stir-fry until just cooked through (about 3–5 minutes for chicken, less for shrimp). Remove the protein from the pan and set it aside to prevent overcooking while you cook the vegetables.
  5. In the same pan, add the harder vegetables first — the carrot, cauliflower, and broccoli — and stir-fry for 2–3 minutes so they begin to soften. If the pan looks dry, splash a tablespoon of broth or oil to keep everything moving and prevent sticking.
  6. Add bell peppers, snow peas, cabbage, and baby corn to the pan and continue stir-frying quickly over high heat to keep them crisp-tender. Cook for another 2 minutes until the cabbage wilts slightly but still retains some crunch.
  7. Pour the prepared sauce into the pan and bring it to a gentle simmer so the flavors mingle with the vegetables. Taste and adjust with a little more soy or fish sauce if you prefer it saltier.
  8. Stir in the cornstarch slurry slowly while stirring so the sauce thickens to a glossy consistency and coats the vegetables. Return the cooked protein to the pan and toss everything together for 1–2 minutes until heated through and evenly coated.
  9. Turn off the heat and season with a grind of black pepper and, if desired, a tiny splash of sesame oil for aroma. Give it one final toss and transfer to a serving plate to serve immediately with steamed rice.
